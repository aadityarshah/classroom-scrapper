Linear Transformations and Matrix Representation​

A function T:Rn→RmT: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^mT:Rn→Rm is a linear transformation. Given the standard basis B={e1,…,en}B = \{e_1, \ldots, e_n\}B={e1​,…,en​} of Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn, the matrix representation of TTT with respect to BBB, denoted [T]B[T]_B[T]B​, is an m×nm \times nm×n matrix whose columns are the images of the basis vectors under TTT: [T]B=[T(e1) T(e2) … T(en)][T]_B = [T(e_1) \ T(e_2) \ \ldots \ T(e_n)][T]B​=[T(e1​) T(e2​) … T(en​)]. For any vector x∈Rnx \in \mathbb{R}^nx∈Rn, T(x)T(x)T(x) can be computed as [T]Bx[T]_B x[T]B​x.

Kernel of a Linear Transformation​

Definition: The kernel of a linear transformation T:Rn→RmT: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^mT:Rn→Rm, denoted ker(T)\text{ker}(T)ker(T), is the set of all vectors x∈Rnx \in \mathbb{R}^nx∈Rn such that T(x)=0T(x) = 0T(x)=0. The kernel is a vector subspace of Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn, corresponding to the solution space of the homogeneous linear system [T]Bx=0[T]_B x = 0[T]B​x=0.

Image of a Linear Transformation​

Definition: The image of a linear transformation T:Rn→RmT: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^mT:Rn→Rm, denoted im(T)\text{im}(T)im(T), is the set of all vectors y∈Rmy \in \mathbb{R}^my∈Rm such that y=T(x)y = T(x)y=T(x) for some x∈Rnx \in \mathbb{R}^nx∈Rn. The image is a vector subspace of Rm\mathbb{R}^mRm and is spanned by the images of the standard basis vectors, i.e., im(T)=span{T(e1),…,T(en)}\text{im}(T) = \text{span}\{T(e_1), \ldots, T(e_n)\}im(T)=span{T(e1​),…,T(en​)}.

Nullity and Rank​

Definition: The nullity of a linear transformation TTT, denoted nullity(T)\text{nullity}(T)nullity(T), is the dimension of its kernel: nullity(T)=dim(ker(T))\text{nullity}(T) = \text{dim}(\text{ker}(T))nullity(T)=dim(ker(T)). Definition: The rank of a linear transformation TTT, denoted rank(T)\text{rank}(T)rank(T), is the dimension of its image: rank(T)=dim(im(T))\text{rank}(T) = \text{dim}(\text{im}(T))rank(T)=dim(im(T)). The rank of TTT is equal to the rank of its matrix representation [T]B[T]_B[T]B​.

Rank-Nullity Theorem​

Theorem (Rank-Nullity Theorem): For a linear transformation T:Rn→RmT: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^mT:Rn→Rm, the sum of its rank and nullity equals the dimension of its domain. rank(T)+nullity(T)=n\text{rank}(T) + \text{nullity}(T) = nrank(T)+nullity(T)=n

Key Formulas​

  • Matrix Representation: [T]B=[T(e1) T(e2) … T(en)][T]_B = [T(e_1) \ T(e_2) \ \ldots \ T(e_n)][T]B​=[T(e1​) T(e2​) … T(en​)]
  • Kernel Definition: ker(T)={x∈Rn∣T(x)=0}\text{ker}(T) = \{x \in \mathbb{R}^n \mid T(x) = 0\}ker(T)={x∈Rn∣T(x)=0}
  • Image Definition: im(T)={T(x)∣x∈Rn}\text{im}(T) = \{T(x) \mid x \in \mathbb{R}^n\}im(T)={T(x)∣x∈Rn}
  • Nullity: nullity(T)=dim(ker(T))\text{nullity}(T) = \text{dim}(\text{ker}(T))nullity(T)=dim(ker(T))
  • Rank: rank(T)=dim(im(T))\text{rank}(T) = \text{dim}(\text{im}(T))rank(T)=dim(im(T))
  • Rank-Nullity Theorem: rank(T)+nullity(T)=n\text{rank}(T) + \text{nullity}(T) = nrank(T)+nullity(T)=n

Quick Summary​

Linear transformations T:Rn→RmT: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^mT:Rn→Rm can be represented by an m×nm \times nm×n matrix [T]B[T]_B[T]B​. The kernel is the subspace of vectors mapped to zero, and its dimension is the nullity. The image is the subspace of all possible outputs, and its dimension is the rank. The Rank-Nullity Theorem states that for any linear transformation, rank(T)+nullity(T)=n\text{rank}(T) + \text{nullity}(T) = nrank(T)+nullity(T)=n, where nnn is the dimension of the domain.
