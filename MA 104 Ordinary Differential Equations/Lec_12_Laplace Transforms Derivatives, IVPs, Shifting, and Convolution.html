Solving Initial Value Problems (IVPs) using Laplace Transforms​

Methodology:

  1. Apply the Laplace transform to the given Ordinary Differential Equation (ODE).
  2. Use the Laplace transform of derivatives formula and substitute initial conditions at t=0t=0t=0.
  3. Solve the resulting algebraic equation for L{y(t)}\mathcal{L}\{y(t)\}L{y(t)}.
  4. Apply the inverse Laplace transform to find y(t)y(t)y(t).

Quick Summary​

This lecture established key properties of the Laplace transform crucial for solving Ordinary Differential Equations. We covered the Laplace transform of derivatives, enabling the transformation of ODEs into algebraic equations. The Unit Step Function and the Second Shifting Theorem provide tools for handling piecewise continuous functions and time-delayed signals. Properties relating to differentiation and integration of the Laplace transform were introduced, facilitating the transformation of functions multiplied or divided by ttt. Finally, the Convolution Theorem was defined, providing a method for finding the inverse Laplace transform of a product of two functions in the sss-domain. These concepts collectively form a powerful framework for analyzing and solving IVPs.
