Shifting Initial Conditions to the Origin​

Definition: Original Initial Value Problem (IVP)​

Quick Summary​

The technique of shifting initial conditions simplifies solving an IVP by transforming it into an equivalent problem with the initial condition at the origin. The Uniqueness Theorem establishes conditions (continuity and boundedness of fff and ∂f/∂y\partial f/\partial y∂f/∂y) under which an IVP is guaranteed to have at most one solution locally, and these local solutions can sometimes be extended to prove global uniqueness.
