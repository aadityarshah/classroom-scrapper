Quick Summary​

Lecture 4 introduced the fundamental Existence and Uniqueness Theorem for IVPs, stating that a unique solution exists in a local interval under continuity conditions for f(x,y)f(x,y)f(x,y) and its partial derivative ∂f∂y\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}∂y∂f​. The IVP was shown to be equivalent to an integral equation. This equivalence forms the basis for Picard's Iteration Method, a constructive technique that generates a sequence of approximate solutions converging to the true solution. Finally, the uniqueness of the solution was rigorously proven by assuming two solutions, deriving an integral inequality, and using an integrating factor to show that the difference between the solutions must be zero.
